# Aveo CharacterAI: Ideal Customer Profile

## Segment 1: Solo Content Creators / Influencer Aspirants

### Demographics
- Age: 22 to 35
- Gender: 55% male, 45% female
- Income: $30K to $80K annually (side hustle income, entry level jobs, freelancing)
- Location: US, UK, Canada, Australia, India (English speaking markets)
- Education: High school to bachelor's degree, often self taught in content creation
- Tech literacy: Comfortable with apps and online tools, but not developers

### Psychographics
- Dream of building a personal brand or becoming a full time creator
- Obsessed with social media growth metrics (followers, views, engagement)
- Fear of being on camera, or dislike their own appearance/voice
- Drawn to the "faceless content" trend on YouTube and TikTok
- Value speed and ease over perfection
- Follow creator economy influencers like Ali Abdaal, Matt Gray, and Dan Koe
- Active in hustle culture, "build in public," and side income communities
- Highly price sensitive but willing to invest if ROI is clear within 30 days

### Pain Points
1. **Camera anxiety.** They want to create video content but feel uncomfortable or unable to film themselves consistently.
2. **Time poverty.** Between their job and life, they cannot produce 5+ videos per week to satisfy platform algorithms.
3. **Tool overload.** They currently cobble together ChatGPT for scripts, ElevenLabs for voice, HeyGen or D-ID for avatar video, CapCut for editing, and Hootsuite for scheduling. Five or more subscriptions totaling $150+ per month with hours of manual work.
4. **Inconsistency kills growth.** They post for 2 weeks, burn out, go silent for a month, and lose all momentum.
5. **Quality gap.** Their DIY videos look amateur compared to polished creators they admire.

### Desires
- Passive content machine that runs while they sleep
- A unique, recognizable AI character that feels like "their" brand
- Consistent posting schedule without daily effort
- Professional quality output without hiring editors or voice actors
- The ability to test different niches without starting from scratch each time

## Segment 2: Digital Marketing Agencies / Social Media Managers

### Demographics
- Company size: 2 to 25 employees
- Revenue: $200K to $5M annually
- Role of buyer: Agency owner, creative director, or social media manager
- Location: US, UK, Western Europe, Australia
- Industry focus: Social media management, content marketing, performance marketing
- Clients served: 5 to 50 active clients

### Psychographics
- Obsessed with efficiency and margins. Every minute saved is money earned.
- Constantly searching for tools that give them a competitive edge over other agencies
- Under pressure from clients who demand more content for the same retainer
- Early adopters of AI tools. Already using ChatGPT, Midjourney, and Jasper in workflows.
- Value white label and multi client capabilities
- Think in terms of scalable systems, not one off projects
- Frustrated by the hiring market for video editors and content creators

### Pain Points
1. **Client demand outpaces capacity.** Clients want daily video content but the team can only produce 2 to 3 per week per client.
2. **Video production costs are brutal.** Hiring freelance video editors costs $30 to $100 per video. At scale, this destroys margins.
3. **Creative talent is scarce.** Finding reliable video creators who understand brand voice is nearly impossible.
4. **Repetitive work drains team morale.** Writing scripts, editing clips, and uploading to 3 platforms for 15 clients is soul crushing.
5. **Client churn from underdelivering.** When they cannot keep up with content volume promises, clients leave for agencies that can.

### Desires
- A tool that lets one team member manage video content for 10+ clients
- White label output they can present as their own production capability
- Batch production where they set up campaigns and let the system run
- Analytics they can include in client reports without manual compilation
- The ability to sell "AI video content" as a premium service offering with high margins

## Segment 3: E-commerce Brands / DTC Companies

### Demographics
- Company size: 5 to 100 employees
- Revenue: $500K to $20M annually
- Role of buyer: Head of Marketing, CMO, Growth Lead, or DTC founder
- Location: US, UK, Western Europe
- Industry: Consumer goods, beauty, health supplements, fashion, fitness, pet products, food
- Channels: Shopify, Amazon, direct website

### Psychographics
- Performance marketing mindset. Every dollar spent must generate measurable ROI.
- Frustrated by rising Facebook/TikTok CPMs and declining ad performance
- Obsessed with UGC (user generated content) because it converts 2 to 4x better than polished brand ads
- Spending $5K to $30K/mo on paid media and struggling with creative fatigue
- Know they need more ad creative variations but cannot produce them fast enough
- Hiring UGC creators costs $200 to $500 per video and takes weeks of back and forth
- Value speed to market over production perfection

### Pain Points
1. **Creative fatigue kills ROAS.** Their best performing ad creatives stop working after 7 to 14 days. They need fresh variations constantly.
2. **UGC is expensive and slow.** Sourcing UGC creators via Billo, JoinBrands, or direct outreach costs $200 to $500 per video with 2 to 4 week turnaround.
3. **Volume problem.** They need 30 to 50 ad creative variations per month to feed Meta and TikTok algorithms. Current capacity is 5 to 10.
4. **Brand consistency is impossible with freelancers.** Every UGC creator delivers a different vibe, quality level, and messaging accuracy.
5. **No way to A/B test at scale.** Testing hooks, scripts, and CTAs requires producing many variations. At $300/video, testing is prohibitively expensive.

### Desires
- Unlimited ad creative variations on demand
- 24 to 48 hour turnaround from concept to published ad
- Consistent "character spokesperson" that audiences recognize and trust
- The ability to test 10 hook variations for $0 extra cost
- A dedicated "brand character" that becomes the face of their social presence

### Current Tools and Frustrations
| Tool | What They Use It For | Frustration |

| Billo / JoinBrands | UGC creator marketplace | $200 to $500/video, 2+ week turnaround, inconsistent quality |

| In house team | Some content | Slow, expensive ($60K+ salary), limited by team size |
| Meta Ads Manager | Paid advertising | Creative fatigue, needs constant fresh assets |
| TikTok Ads Manager | Paid TikTok | Same creative fatigue problem |
| Canva / Adobe | Ad design | Not built for video UGC production |

**Total current UGC spend: $3K to $15K/mo for 10 to 30 videos. Often with 2+ week lead times.**
